## Recommended starting FeeParams

```
baseFeeMintSide       = 3000   // 30 bps
baseFeeRedeemSide     = 500    // 5 bps
minFee                = 200
maxFee                = 10000
fallbackFee           = 3000
deviationThresholdPpm = 1000   // 10 bps
toxicitySlopePpm      = 500000 // 0.5x
surchargeCapPpm       = 6000   // 60 bps
ethUsdStalenessSec    = 4500
gbpUsdStalenessSec    = 90000
```

Justification from the tables above:
- **Nonzero slope is the whole game**: every slope>0 config beats both baselines in both regimes; slope=0 configs lose to even the static-30bps baseline (the directional bases alone under-tax informed flow). The surcharge component is ~60-70% of total fee revenue at slope>=0.5.
- **(30,5) bases**: top-2 worst-case rank across regimes, statistically tied with (25,10); (30,5) is preferred because it preserves exact band symmetry (mint = redeem + the wrapper's 25 bps redeem leg, spec section 2) and taxes organic mint-side flow harder, which the tables show is where base revenue comes from.
- **Slope 0.5x, not 1.0x, at launch**: 1.0x ranks first in the raw tables, but the sim's single-fill arb agent pays top-of-ramp surcharges that a splitting searcher erodes toward the schedule integral (verified in SECURITY_WETH_WSTGBP.md section 1 - the 1.0x edge is inflated by exactly the revenue splitting removes). 0.5x is within ~2 ranks in both regimes, matches the spec's start-conservative directive, and the owner can retune upward from live SwapFee telemetry.
- **Range-width caveat (POL policy, not FeeParams)**: the +-75% static range spent ~29% of the 2021 x3 trend out of range (breach bars column) - fee revenue stops while breached. Chop-2024 never breached. Wider ranges trade fee density for trend coverage; revisit position width, not fee params, if trend regimes dominate.
- Baselines: recommended config beats static-30bps and the Curve-approx (50% leakage) on POL PnL vs benchmark in both regimes by wide margins.
